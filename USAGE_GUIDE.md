## Your First Trade (Paper Mode)

Paper trading uses fake money so you can practice without risk.

### Step 1: Go to Paper Trading

1. Click on **Paper Trading** in the left sidebar
2. You'll see your virtual cash balance (usually $100,000 to start)

### Step 2: Find a Stock to Buy

**Option A: Use the Watchlist**

1. Go to **Market** page
2. Search for a company you know (e.g., "AAPL" for Apple)
3. Click "Add to Watchlist"
4. View the signals for that stock

**Option B: Use AI Recommendations**

1. Go to **Trading** page
2. Look at the signals list
3. Find a "BUY" signal with high confidence (>70%)

### Step 3: Understanding the Signal

Before buying, check these details:

**Signal Type**:

- ðŸŸ¢ BUY = AI suggests buying
- ðŸ”´ SELL = AI suggests selling (if you own it)
- ðŸŸ¡ HOLD = AI suggests keeping what you have

**Confidence**: How sure the AI is (0-100%)

- 90-100%: Very confident
- 70-89%: Confident
- 50-69%: Moderate confidence
- Below 50%: Low confidence (probably skip)

**Target Price**: Price AI expects stock to reach
**Stop Loss**: Safety price - sell if it drops this low

**Example Signal**:

```
AAPL - BUY
Confidence: 85%
Current Price: $175
Target Price: $190
Stop Loss: $165
```

This means:

- Buy Apple stock now at $175
- AI expects it to reach $190
- If it drops to $165, consider selling to limit loss

### Step 4: Place Your Order

1. Click on the signal or stock
2. Click "Trade" or "Buy" button
3. Enter number of shares (start with 1-10)
4. Review your order
5. Click "Confirm"

**Order Types**:

- **Market Order**: Buy immediately at current price (easiest for beginners)
- **Limit Order**: Only buy at a specific price you set (more control)

### Step 5: Monitor Your Trade

1. Go to **Portfolio** page
2. See your new position listed
3. Watch the price over time
4. Check back daily to see how it's doing

### Step 6: When to Sell

Sell when:

- âœ… You reach your target price (profit!)
- âœ… You get a SELL signal with high confidence
- âœ… Price drops to your stop loss (limit loss)
- âš ï¸ You need to rebalance your portfolio

**Don't sell based on emotions!** Stick to your plan.

## Managing Your Portfolio

### Viewing Your Positions

**Portfolio Page** shows:

- All stocks you own
- How many shares
- What you paid (cost basis)
- Current value
- Profit or loss for each

### Understanding Portfolio Metrics

**Simple Metrics**:

- **Total Value**: What your portfolio is worth now
- **Total Cost**: What you originally paid
- **Total Gain/Loss**: Difference (profit or loss)
- **Daily Change**: How much changed today
- **% Return**: Gain/loss as percentage

**Advanced Metrics** (with â„¹ï¸ help icons):

- **Sharpe Ratio**: How good your returns are vs. risk (higher is better)
- **Beta**: How volatile compared to market (1.0 = average)
- **Alpha**: How much you beat the market (positive is good)
- **Max Drawdown**: Biggest loss from peak (smaller is better)

**Hover over the â„¹ï¸ icons** next to each metric to see detailed explanations!

### Portfolio Health Check

**Good Signs** (Green):

- âœ… Diversified (many different stocks)
- âœ… Positive total return
- âœ… Low volatility
- âœ… Good Sharpe ratio (>1.0)

**Warning Signs** (Yellow/Red):

- âš ï¸ Too concentrated (>20% in one stock)
- âš ï¸ High volatility
- âš ï¸ Many losing positions
- âš ï¸ Negative total return

### Rebalancing Your Portfolio

**When to Rebalance**:

- One stock becomes >25% of portfolio
- You have too many (>30) or too few (<5) stocks
- Risk level doesn't match your goals

**How to Rebalance**:

1. Sell some of your biggest positions
2. Buy more of underrepresented sectors
3. Follow AI recommendations for diversification

### Common Questions

**Q: Can I lose real money?**
A: Not in Paper Trading mode! You're using fake money. When you're ready for real trading, you'll need to connect a real brokerage account.

**Q: How accurate are the AI signals?**
A: No system is 100% accurate. Treat signals as suggestions, not guarantees. The confidence score helps you judge reliability.

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**
A: In Paper Trading, mistakes don't matter - it's practice! You can always reset your paper portfolio.

**Q: How much should I invest?**
A: Start small! In paper mode, practice with different amounts. In real trading, only invest what you can afford to lose.

**Q: Do I need to watch the market all day?**
A: No! Set up alerts for important price movements and check in a few times per day.

**Q: What does [term] mean?**
A: Click on **Help & Glossary** in the sidebar and search for any term you don't understand.

## Best Practices for Beginners

### Start Safe

1. **Use Paper Trading First**
   - Practice until you're comfortable
   - Try different strategies
   - Learn from mistakes without cost

2. **Start Small**
   - When you go to real trading, start with small amounts
   - Buy 1-10 shares, not 100
   - Build confidence gradually

3. **Diversify**
   - Don't put all money in one stock
   - Aim for 10-20 different stocks
   - Spread across different industries

### Make Smart Decisions

1. **Read the Signals Carefully**
   - Check confidence scores
   - Read the reasoning
   - Look at price targets and stop losses

2. **Set Stop Losses**
   - Protect yourself from big losses
   - Automatically sell if price drops too much
   - Think of it as insurance

3. **Don't Chase Performance**
   - Don't buy just because price went up
   - Don't panic sell on small drops
   - Stick to your plan

### Learn Continuously

1. **Use the Help Features**
   - Click all the â„¹ï¸ icons
   - Read term definitions
   - Understand what you're doing

2. **Track Your Trades**
   - Keep notes on why you bought/sold
   - Learn from both wins and losses
   - Improve over time

3. **Stay Informed**
   - Read the news feed
   - Understand market conditions
   - Know what affects your stocks
